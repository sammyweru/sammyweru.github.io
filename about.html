The Journey So Far And Where I'm Headed

My name is Samuel Chege Waweru. I'm a self-taught cybersecurity learner from Kenya with a mission: to protect digital systems, elevate cybersecurity awareness, and become a trusted architect of secure networks in Africa and beyond.

I didn’t start out in tech. I wasn’t handed a roadmap. Everything I’ve learned so far from configuring VLANs to analyzing breach data has come from pure curiosity, structured self-learning, and the determination to build something bigger than myself. I don’t fake expertise. I study hard, break systems ethically, and fix them with purpose.

My journey into cybersecurity began with a simple question: “How do hackers think?” That single question turned into late nights with Wireshark, CEH labs, Packet Tracer topologies, and deep dives into tools like SpiderFoot, Recon-ng, and Shodan. I’ve documented this journey openly not just for myself, but for other Africans looking to enter this field with limited resources but unlimited hunger.

What I'm Learning & Practicing:

  • Simulating enterprise networks (VLANs, OSPF, NAT, ACLs, DHCP, SSH)
  • Ethical hacking through CEH-style labs & OSINT tools like Shodan, h8mail, ExifTool
  • Automating recon with Python & Bash scripting
  • Applying network defense and compliance principles (MITRE ATT&CK, PCI DSS, Kenya Data Protection Act)

I believe cybersecurity isn’t just about tools and firewalls it’s about protecting people. Whether it’s a vulnerable child whose data is exposed, or a small business trying to survive a ransomware attack, I want to be part of the solution. I don’t just want a job in cybersecurity. I want a purpose in it.

I’m not an expert, not yet. But I show up every day. I build, I break, I fix, I document. And with every lab, every GitHub commit, every mistake and breakthrough, I’m getting closer to being the kind of cybersecurity professional Kenya and the world needs.

My Philosophy

  • Learn deeplyNot just theory, but how systems behave in real life
  • Think ethicallySecurity is a responsibility, not just a job
  • Build for impactI want to secure Kenya’s digital future, not just collect certs

If you’re a mentor, recruiter, collaborator or someone walking the same road feel free to reach out. I don’t have all the answers. But I’ll always show up, ask the right questions, and build toward the right ones.
